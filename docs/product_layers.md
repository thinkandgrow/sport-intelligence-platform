# Chat Your Race Product Layers

## Purpose

Chat Your Race is not a single feature.

It is a journey from raw race results to deep understanding.

Each product layer answers a different type of user question.

Together, these layers transform data into knowledge.

---

# Layer 1 — Results

Question:

**What happened?**

Examples:

* What was my finish time?
* What was my pace?
* What place did I finish?
* How many runners finished?

Goal:

Provide accurate race facts.

---

# Layer 2 — Performance

Question:

**How well did I perform?**

Examples:

* Was this a good race?
* Did I pace well?
* Did I finish strongly?
* Was I consistent?

Goal:

Interpret performance rather than simply report results.

---

# Layer 3 — Comparison

Question:

**Compared with whom?**

Examples:

* How did I compare with runners my age?
* Was I above average?
* How did my country perform?
* How competitive was my category?

Goal:

Provide meaningful context.

---

# Layer 4 — Context

Question:

**Why did this happen?**

Examples:

* Did the weather affect my race?
* Did the course profile matter?
* Was congestion a factor?

Goal:

Explain the result through external conditions.

---

# Layer 5 — Improvement

Question:

**What should I improve next?**

Examples:

* What limited my performance?
* What should I focus on in training?
* Which weakness matters most?

Goal:

Turn analysis into actionable advice.

---

# Layer 6 — Research

Question:

**What patterns exist?**

Examples:

* What trends appear across races?
* Which variables matter most?
* Are there hidden relationships in the data?

Goal:

Generate new knowledge through sports analytics.

---

# Layer 7 — Story

Question:

**What does this result mean?**

Examples:

* What story does this race tell?
* What can I learn about myself?
* How has this race changed me?

Goal:

Transform data into understanding.

This is the highest layer of the product.

It reflects the philosophy:

> Every result has a story. Chat it.

---

## Product Evolution

The first versions of Chat Your Race will focus on Layers 1 and 2.

Each future release will expand into higher layers.

The long-term vision is not to display race results.

The long-term vision is to help people understand themselves through sport.
